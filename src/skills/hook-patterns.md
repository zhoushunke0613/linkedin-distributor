# Hook patterns with examples

The 6 hook types aren't interchangeable — each does a specific job.

## data_shock
Lead with a number that reframes the topic. The number must be **specific,
nontrivial, and surprising.**

- ✅ "30% of your Meta conversions aren't Meta's. Here's how we proved it."
- ❌ "Attribution is hard for most marketers." (no number)
- ❌ "Many companies struggle with tracking." (vague)

## pain_resonance
Describe a pain the reader has felt but not named. Use their vocabulary, not
yours. First sentence = the feeling; second = the diagnosis.

- ✅ "Your Meta dashboard says 4x ROAS. Your bank account disagrees. You're
  not wrong — but you are being lied to."

## counterintuitive
Take a belief the audience holds and invert it. The inversion must be
**defensible**, not just contrarian for its own sake.

- ✅ "Attribution isn't hard. It's wrong. Here's the difference."
- ❌ "Everyone is wrong about marketing." (too broad, no claim)

## story_opener
Open in a specific scene — a conversation, a moment, a realization.
Anchor the reader in time and place before zooming out to the lesson.

- ✅ "Tuesday, 11pm. Our CMO pinged: 'Meta ROAS dropped 40% overnight. Fix
  it.' It wasn't broken. It was finally honest."

## listicle
"N things / N lessons / N mistakes." Works because it promises a discrete
scannable payload. N should be odd and < 8.

- ✅ "3 attribution myths that cost us $50K in Q1."

## question
Open with a question the reader actively wants answered. Must be something
they'd Google, not something rhetorical.

- ✅ "Why does iOS 17 crush your click-through conversions but not your
  view-through?" 
- ❌ "Have you ever wondered about attribution?" (rhetorical)
