# Content goals

Clarify what the post is supposed to accomplish before writing begins.

## When to use

- At the start of post planning.
- When the topic is clear but the communication objective is not.
- When posts feel unfocused or overstuffed.

## Inputs

user_request / topic / optional_target_audience / optional_funnel_context

## Outputs

selected_primary_goal / optional_secondary_goal / emphasis_guidance / avoidance_guidance

## Workflow

1. Infer the real communication objective.
2. Choose one primary goal only.
3. Optionally choose one secondary goal if it naturally supports the first.
4. State what the post should emphasize.
5. State what the post should avoid.

## Guardrails

- Do not combine too many goals.
- Do not confuse engagement with strategic positioning.
- Do not force direct conversion goals into every post.

## Quality bar

- The chosen goal should make the next writing decisions easier.
- The post should behave consistently with the selected goal.

## Goal reference

### brand_awareness
- emphasize: Brand-shaped perspective; memorable framing
- avoid: Over-explaining product details

### pain_point_agitation
- emphasize: Pain; consequences; misdiagnosis
- avoid: Solution overload too early

### category_education
- emphasize: Clarifying how the problem actually works; reframing assumptions
- avoid: Aggressive selling

### product_positioning
- emphasize: What Attribuly uniquely helps clarify or recover
- avoid: Feature dumping

### objection_handling
- emphasize: Why the existing stack may still leave blind spots
- avoid: Defensive tone

### competitor_reframing
- emphasize: Category truth; decision logic
- avoid: Petty attacks

### conversation_starting
- emphasize: Strong angle; thoughtful close
- avoid: Overly dense detail

### thought_leadership
- emphasize: Operator observation; commercial insight
- avoid: Motivational fluff
