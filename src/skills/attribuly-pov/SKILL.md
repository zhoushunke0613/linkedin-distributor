# Attribuly POV

Inject Attribuly's distinct worldview into the post so the content builds
brand recognition, not just engagement.

## When to use

- When a post risks sounding like generic ecommerce advice.
- When the draft needs stronger brand framing.
- When category education or positioning is central.

## Inputs

topic / target_audience / content_goal / topic_cluster / draft_angle

## Outputs

selected_pov_statement / supporting_brand_frames /
optional_reframe_line / optional_product_connection_line

## Workflow

1. Choose the Attribuly worldview that best matches the topic.
2. Reframe the topic through that worldview.
3. Write one concise POV statement.
4. Add 2 to 4 supporting practical frames.
5. Suggest a low-pressure path from worldview to product relevance.

## Guardrails

- Do not pile multiple POVs into one post.
- Do not use generic SaaS thought leadership language.
- Do not force product mention if the POV is stronger without it.
- Do not make unsupported superiority claims.

## Quality bar

- The post should feel like a specific worldview, not generic advice.
- The POV should be commercially meaningful.
- The wording should stay clear and operator-friendly.

## POV library

- Most brands are not only losing on acquisition. They are losing on recovery and visibility after the click.
- Attribution is not just a reporting issue. It shapes real budget and scaling decisions.
- Retention tools can only act on users they can actually identify.
- More dashboards do not automatically create more clarity.
- The real problem is often not a weak flow, but weak visibility into who can enter the flow.
- Seeing revenue is not the same as recovering revenue.
