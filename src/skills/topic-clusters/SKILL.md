# Topic clusters

Keep content anchored to repeatable Attribuly themes instead of random one-off ideas.

## When to use

- When the raw topic is vague.
- When maintaining long-term brand consistency matters.
- Before POV and structure selection.

## Inputs

raw_idea / target_audience / content_goal

## Outputs

selected_topic_cluster / reason_it_fits / optional_adjacent_angles

## Workflow

1. Map the raw idea to one core Attribuly topic cluster.
2. Reject off-brand tangents.
3. Suggest adjacent future angles if useful.
4. Keep the content engine focused on repeatable themes.

## Guardrails

- Do not choose multiple clusters unless clearly necessary.
- Do not use overly broad category labels.
- Do not force-fit unrelated ideas.

## Quality bar

- The chosen cluster should feel obviously relevant.
- The cluster should help future content stay cumulative.

## Cluster reference

- **anonymous_traffic** — Traffic exists, but many visitors are still not identifiable.
- **identity_resolution** — The ability to identify and connect otherwise hidden visitors and actions.
- **attribution_trust** — Confidence in what actually drove revenue and where budget should go.
- **hidden_revenue_leakage** — Revenue loss caused by partial visibility or weak recovery.
- **abandoned_cart_gap** — The difference between carts abandoned and carts actually recoverable.
- **email_flow_underperformance** — When email automation underdelivers because upstream inputs are weak.
- **paid_traffic_inefficiency** — Paid acquisition spend that is not fully captured or recoverable.
- **dashboard_overload** — Too many reports, too little useful clarity.
- **decision_clarity** — The need for cleaner, more actionable understanding.
- **revenue_recovery** — Recovering value from traffic or intent that already exists.
- **platform_blind_spots** — What tools cannot see, match, or action effectively.
