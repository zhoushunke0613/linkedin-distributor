# Engagement patterns

Design natural LinkedIn endings that increase comments, disagreement,
saves, or DMs without sounding manipulative.

## When to use

- When a post needs a stronger closing.
- When the user wants engagement without sounding like engagement bait.
- After the main body direction is established.

## Inputs

topic / audience / content_goal / post_tone / post_type

## Outputs

- **closing_options** — 3 closing candidates
- **closing_labels** — one of:
  operator_question / debate_prompt / pattern_check / soft_cta /
  disagreement_invite / experience_prompt

## Workflow

1. Identify what kind of response the post should invite.
2. Match the close to the tone and objective of the post.
3. Keep the close conversational and credible.
4. Prefer responses that invite operator-level discussion.
5. Avoid cheap or repetitive engagement tricks.

## Guardrails

- Do not always end with "agree or disagree?"
- Do not use fake curiosity.
- Do not tack on a CTA that breaks the tone.
- Do not make every post ask for a demo or DM.

## Quality bar

- The close should feel like a natural continuation of the post.
- The reader should feel invited, not manipulated.
- The closing style should match the post's seriousness and tone.

## Examples of closing styles

- "Curious how many teams are seeing this in their own setup."
- "Is this something you're seeing too, or is your stack handling it better?"
- "I'd be interested to hear whether others see this as a reporting issue or a visibility issue first."
