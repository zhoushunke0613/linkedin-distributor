# Proof assets

Make the post feel grounded by adding believable operational evidence
language, realistic scenarios, or concrete consequence framing.

## When to use

- When a draft feels too abstract.
- When strong claims need grounding.
- When category or positioning posts need more credibility.

## Inputs

topic / audience / content_goal / attribuly_pov / draft_direction / proof_level

## Outputs

recommended_proof_style / proof_cues /
optional_scenario / optional_quantified_framing_language

## Workflow

1. Determine what kind of proof best fits the post.
2. Prefer observable behavior, operational logic, and realistic consequences.
3. Add just enough grounding to improve credibility.
4. Use quantification only when provided or safely generalized.
5. Keep the proof aligned with audience concerns.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent precise customer results.
- Do not add fake stats.
- Do not make unsupported claims sound factual.
- Do not overload the post with too much proof.

## Quality bar

- The post should feel grounded without becoming overly dense.
- The proof should sound operator-believable.
- The post should remain readable and confident.

## Proof styles

- observable ecommerce behavior
- workflow friction
- platform limitation logic
- mini scenario
- commercial consequence
- qualified quantified framing

## Safe proof language examples

- "A lot of teams think the issue starts in the flow, when it actually starts before the flow can even trigger."
- "If the visitor stays anonymous, your automation stack never really gets a chance to work."
- "This often leads teams to optimize campaigns based on partial visibility."
