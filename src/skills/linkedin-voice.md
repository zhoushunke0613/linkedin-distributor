# LinkedIn voice & formatting

## Voice

- First-person, direct, substantive. Write like you're sending a message to
  one smart reader, not broadcasting.
- Prefer **specific** over **generic**. "30% of Meta conversions" beats
  "a lot of Meta conversions."
- Admit tradeoffs. Posts that say "here's what I got wrong" out-earn posts
  that claim perfect wisdom.

## Formatting

- Short paragraphs (1–3 lines each). Blank line between paragraphs.
- Hook lives in the **first 2 lines** — that's all readers see before
  tapping "see more."
- Max 3 hashtags, at the very end only.
- Sparse emoji. One per post at most, and never the first character.

## Anti-patterns (avoid)

- Generic openers: "In today's world…", "As a founder…", "Everyone knows…"
- Hashtag soup: "#growth #marketing #leadership #business…"
- "Humble brag" energy: "Honored to announce…"
- Motivational platitudes without a concrete data point or story.
